Interlochen center for the arts

The Interlochen Center for the Arts has programs for third grade students to adults. Campus tours are available in from last week of June through the beginning of August. Visitors are welcome to walk the campus and maps are available at their Scholarship store.

Betsie river

Betsie River flows through Benzie, Manistee, and Grand Traverse counties. Starting from Green Lake, in Interlochen, and flows west into Betsie Lake and Lake Michigan around the Frankfort/Elberta area. Bestie River contains 93 river miles and 53 of them are mainsteam.

Interlochen

According to Wikipedia

Members of the Odawa tribe lived near what is now Duck Lake and Green Lake before European settlers arrived. Logging and fishing industries were developed in this area by European settlers in the late nineteenth century. Interlochen State Park was developed in 1917, which was the first state park in Michigan, to help preserve the remaining forests in the area. The National High School Orchestra Camp was founded in 1928, which later evolved into The Interlochen Center for the Arts.
